The Challenges of In-House Data Centres

High Capital and Operational Expenses

Space can be limited on your on-site infrastructure and often comes with heavy investments in staff, systems and security, which only grow as you do. In addition to the upfront setup costs, continued maintenance, energy bills, and upgrades can strain your organisation’s budgets.

Security and Compliance Burdens

Most organisations will find that maintaining round-the-clock physical and network security is an uphill task. Your on-premises security may lack the layers of protection and compliance certifications that enterprise-grade colocation centres specialise in.

Power and Cooling Challenges

High uptime depends on uninterrupted power availability and climate control, which can only be achieved in a state-of-the-art infrastructural setup. Without a professional-grade infrastructure, your in-house setup is prone to power outages and cooling system failures, with an increased likelihood of service disruptions.

Limited Scalability

You may have set up your on-premises infrastructure just fine, but the real challenge lies in scaling it as your business grows. You’ll need more space, more power, and better cooling systems, all of which require money and time, when you want to add more servers. Colocation services let you scale rapidly without worrying about any infrastructural limitations.

Staff Availability and 24/7 Monitoring

Your on-premises data centre needs round-the-clock monitoring, maintenance and emergency response. You’re at risk of missing alerts or experiencing long downtimes without skilled IT professionals monitoring your servers 24/7.

Low Geo-Redundancy

Catastrophic regional events like power outages, fires, or floods have the potential to harm your IT systems, hindering business continuity and disaster recovery. Colocation hosting providers have multiple geographical sites, so your business can get back up and running in no time.

Key Features of London Colocation Data Centres

Some businesses are content running their operations on public clouds, while many others need or want to have their private IT infrastructure. When the choice lies between running your in-house, private data centre vs using colocation, which one is best for you?

Here are eight attractive features of colocation hosting for your business.

Strong Physical Infrastructure

 

Data centres have cutting-edge IT infrastructure that supports optimal performance. These include redundant power systems, backup generators, advanced cooling systems, fire suppression systems, and physical security measures, including access control, surveillance cameras, and 24/7 monitoring.

 

Reliable Power Supply

 

Colocation data centre providers prioritise uninterrupted power by maximising redundant power feeds, uninterruptible power supply (UPS) and backup generators.

 

Scalable and Flexible

 

As your business grows, colocation data centres support your scaling needs. You can expand your infrastructure within the colocation data facility by adding more rack space or upgrading to bigger colocation hosting options, still avoiding the cost and hassle of building or renovating an in-house data centre. 

 

High-Speed Network Connectivity

 

Most London hosting centres have reliable, high-speed internet connectivity with multiple network carriers. Your servers and networking equipment benefit from high-speed connections with nearly zero latency.

 

Bolstered Security

 

Security is another top priority for colocation providers in the UK. To safeguard sensitive data and protect against unauthorised access or physical threats, UK colocation providers install advanced security systems with features such as surveillance cameras, biometric access controls, locked cabinets, and restricted entry. 

 

Cost-Efficient

 

With accessible colocation in the UK, you can rule out the need to invest heavily in building and maintaining an in-house data centre. Your organisation can benefit from shared infrastructure and operational costs, which are distributed among multiple businesses. This cost-sharing model makes co-location a more affordable option, allowing you to redirect your in-house data centre investment to other aspects of your business.

 

Professional Hands-On Support

 

You’ll receive professional hands-on support from colocation service providers for all kinds of tasks, from server reboots to equipment replacement. This assistance should help you lower downtime and run your IT infrastructure smoothly without being physically present.

 

Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity

 

Organisations are susceptible to disruptions that affect the continuity of operations. Colocation hosting centres make the most of geographically diverse locations, with the strategic placement of power systems and backup mechanisms. This framework makes London data centres like BlackBox Hosting a dependable colocation data centre for disaster recovery and business continuity planning.

If you want to retain ownership and control over your servers without the high costs of building and maintaining them, all while ensuring maximum uptime and superlative performance, BlackBox Hosting’s colocation services are for you.

Colocation FAQs

What is colocation?

Colocation is a service where you receive rack space in a data centre for your server and IT hardware.

What is the difference between a data centre and a colocation centre?

A data centre is a facility that houses servers, networking and IT equipment, while a colocation centre rents out physical space for your organisation to keep its equipment, and the data centre supplies the connectivity, power, cooling and security.

What are the different types of colocation?

Rack space, cage, private suite, wholesale, cloud, and edge are the most common types of colocation.

Is your colocation service suitable for high-security workloads?

Yes, BlackBox Hosting is ISO 27001 certified and operates in Tier 3+ facilities with 24/7 surveillance, badge access, and anti-tailgating measures.

Can I upgrade from colocation to fully managed hosting later?

Yes, we offer seamless migration to managed hosting or private cloud services if you outgrow a co-located setup.
