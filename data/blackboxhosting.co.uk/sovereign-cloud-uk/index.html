What is a Sovereign Cloud?

Sovereign cloud is a type of cloud infrastructure that’s fully developed and operated within the jurisdictional boundaries of a specific nation or geographical region, with the data complying with the nation’s laws and governance.

When your data is stored in a sovereign cloud, you’re guaranteed complete legal, operational and digital sovereignty.

Data Residency Versus Data Sovereignty

 

  • Data residency states where your data is stored. It ensures your data is stored securely in a defined geographic location.
  • Data sovereignty states which nation’s laws govern your data. It guarantees storage in a specific location and is subject to the laws of that nation.

If you host your data in a UK data centre owned by an overseas provider, it may still be subject to foreign laws.

BlackBox Hosting ensures both data residency and sovereignty. Your data resides on UK soil and is legally protected under stringent UK law and regulations, managed by a UK-owned and operated firm.

UK Sovereign Cloud Hosting FAQs

What’s the difference between private cloud hosting and sovereign cloud hosting?

Private cloud hosting offers a dedicated cloud environment for a single organisation with full control, customisation and security. In comparison, sovereign cloud hosting keeps your data secure within a specific geographical boundary, complying with various regulations. 

Why do I need a sovereign cloud?

Ideally, you need a sovereign cloud if you want to protect sensitive data and meet strict security requirements, as well as if you want more control over your data and to comply with regulations.
