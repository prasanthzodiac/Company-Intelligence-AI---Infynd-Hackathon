What is Application Publishing and Why Do You Need It?

Many companies host their software on the Cloud.

Public Clouds are shared, which gives you less control, unpredictable and opaque bills and a reliance on their support team’s availability if anything goes wrong. You’re also entirely responsible for your own data, applications and services, and the extra costs that come with it.

Private Clouds, on the other hand, are 100% dedicated to your business. But hosting them in-house can be expensive, time-intensive and, if not configured correctly, a massive security risk.

We take the stress away by designing, hosting and managing everything for you.

Built on dedicated resources with simple, transparent billing and round-the-clock expert support, our solutions can be deployed in your own data centre, a partner data centre or a secure BlackBox Hosting Tier 3+ facility, allowing you to remain in control of your resources and costs at all times.

Application publishing allows your business to host software on a centralised platform and securely deliver it to your customers or employees over the internet or a private network. This means you don’t need to install locally on every device, nor manage installation across dozens or hundreds of your desktops. With an application publishing or an application delivery solution, you can access the latest version of the software through a secure virtual interface.

Consider these solutions as virtual software programmes that look and act like local applications but are actually running on a server.

Desktop as a Service (DaaS) vs Application Publishing

Application publishing delivers specific applications to the user’s device. Users can quickly and easily interact with the remote applications they need, and not with a complete desktop environment. These apps appear as if they’re running locally.

Desktop as a Service, or DaaS, on the other hand, provides users with a complete virtual desktop. You can use any device to log into a desktop with an operating system, apps, and settings from the cloud. You can run multiple apps and customise your desktop just like a regular computer.

BlackBox Hosting provides both DaaS and application delivery services.
