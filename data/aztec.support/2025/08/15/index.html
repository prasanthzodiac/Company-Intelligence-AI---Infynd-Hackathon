Managing your IT systems can be a daunting task, especially with the rapid adoption of work-from-home and the increasing importance of mobile devices. The complexity and cost can be overwhelming. But there’s a solution that can bring relief. RMM: Remote Monitoring & Management.

What is RMM?

An RMM, or Remote Monitoring and Management, enables your service partner to oversee the daily operations of your IT infrastructure. It ensures your devices stay secure by tracking critical updates and applying proactive measures to guard against threats—whether they originate inside or outside your organisation. A variety of companies offer a range of RMM tools with different features and functionality, which will reduce the need for on-site attendance and minimise the time your employees spend away from their desks for IT-related tasks. Be sure to ask your service partner which they use and what its capabilities are to ensure it’s the right fit!

A Brief History of IT Support.

Before the widespread adoption of RMM tools, Businesses may have relied on an internal IT department – resulting in higher costs, more downtime, and an outdated, reactive approach known as the ‘break-fix’ model. 

The ‘break-fix’ model involved waiting for systems to fail before taking action to repair them. During this time, employees were often unable to work while IT teams investigated the issue, tested solutions, and eventually implemented a fix. This approach was doubly expensive – both in terms of lost productivity and the cost of maintaining a larger internal IT team. With the ever-present issue of increased costs and the rapid introduction of remote work due to Covid, working this way is becoming less and less feasible.

If your business is without an IT Partner or RMM solution, it’s likely that you’re still operating, at least in part, under this outdated model, costing you time and money.

How RMM supports your Service Partner and your Business

Aztec uses RMM tools for update and patch monitoring across a variety of systems – from access points to routers, firewalls, and Windows servers and computers. By using RMM tools, clients are rarely interrupted. These vital tasks are performed helping to mitigate loss of productivity, time, and outages. These tools are crucial for Aztec as we work with you to troubleshoot issues. All of our managed clients are taking full advantage of RMM, fostering a stronger IT management solution and support experience for our clients.

But what if something breaks?

If disaster does strike, RMM tools enable technicians to take immediate remote action to investigate and diagnose issues. Even when an on-site technician is required, they are already informed and prepared to remediate the problem quickly, minimising both disruption and repair costs.

What if nothing goes wrong? Well, no news is often good news, but that doesn’t mean nothing is happening. RMM tools provide a proactive approach to IT management. They monitor your IT infrastructure, giving early warnings of potential issues and allowing for resolution before they impact your business. This proactive nature of RMM can give you peace of mind that your IT is in good hands and being looked after.

How Secure is an RMM?

A significant concern in the tech industry is that of security and visibility over your systems. A remote window into your network, while incredibly useful for your IT team, could be equally valuable for bad actors. The use of an RMM should be clearly outlined and agreed upon by you and any reputable service partner. 

Safeguards that Aztec use include:

  • Consent to control: You are required to approve remote control of a technician before they are allowed access
  • Encryption: Connections are encrypted end-to-end, so nobody can snoop on the session
  • Kill-Switch: An easy and quick way to kill any remote session
  • Multi-Factor Authentication: All Technicians accounts are protected by multiple authentication methods
  • Auditing: Technicians activities are logged, and monitored
These controls are implemented to ensure data security, to help you keep control over your devices while still providing the benefits of the RMM.

An IT support system that scales as you do

RMMs are scalable with your business, they are licensed on a per user or device basis and can be upscaled or downscaled to match the needs of your business. Communication with your service provider is key to ensure this runs smoothly and efficiently so they are up to date on the requirements of your business. However deployment of new RMM agents for additional devices and automated scripts for simple tasks and maintenance means no matter how big your business grows your RMM tool will be able to meet the challenge.

How Aztec Can Help Your Business, Today and for the Future

Nearly half (44%) of UK workers are working in either a fully remote or a hybrid capacity, with this figure only expected to rise. 

In an increasingly remote work world, RMMs give control not only to service providers but also to Business Leaders to keep control over their IT infrastructure, ensuring security and stability in their systems with a modern approach and keeping costs down.

Desktop computers now only make up 39% of devices in the workplace in the UK, while Laptops vastly outpace them. RMMs can support both of these. It’s Important that your employees get support wherever they are, and their IT needs are taken care of.
