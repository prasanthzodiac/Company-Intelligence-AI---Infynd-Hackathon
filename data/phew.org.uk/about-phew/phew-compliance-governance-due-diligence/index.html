Phew Compliance, Governance & Due Diligence

At Phew, safeguarding the data of our clients, employees, and stakeholders is paramount. We are dedicated to upholding the confidentiality and security of the information entrusted to us for processing. In light of the increasing prevalence of data breaches worldwide and evolving compliance regulations, we continuously assess and enhance our data protection measures. 

To ensure compliance with relevant data protection laws, Phew conducts thorough due diligence and adheres to various regulatory and legal frameworks, both mandatory and voluntary. We also actively engage with accreditation schemes to reinforce our data security commitment. Below, we outline the methods through which we prioritise data protection and compliance.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a thorough European privacy law implemented on May 25, 2018. Phew embraces this regulation as a significant step towards aligning data protection standards across the European Union and as an opportunity to assess our current commitments to data protection. We are committed to implementing robust and best-practice data protection measures in compliance with the GDPR. Phew has taken advantage of the introduction of GDPR to align existing policies, procedures, and practices with GDPR requirements and industry best practices. 

UK-Based Hosting, Data Storage & Processing

Phew exclusively utilises data storage facilities situated within the UK for all customer data, ensuring onshore data storage. Phew selects solely UK-based zones, regions, and environments for all cloud-based storage.  

Additionally, Phew presently does not have any agreements to transfer or sub-process data to any company outside of the United Kingdom. 

ISO/IEC 9001:2015 & ISO/IEC 27001:2013 Accreditation

Phew proudly holds both ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 27001:2013 certifications, forming an integrated management system that ensures the highest standards of quality and information security. 

ISO 27001 is a globally recognised standard for information security management, ensuring secure management of office sites, development centres, support centres, and data centres. These certifications undergo renewal audits every three years, along with annual surveillance audits to maintain compliance. 

ISO 9001 is an international standard focused on quality management principles to ensure businesses maintain the quality of processes, products, and services. It emphasises customer focus, involvement of top management, process-oriented approaches, and commitment to continuous improvement. 

We are annually assessed through internal and external audits to uphold both ISO 9001:2015 & ISO 27001:2013 accreditation.  

You can view our certificate here. 

ISO 14001:2015 & ISO 14068-1:2023 Accreditation + Carbon Neutrality

Phew proudly holds ISO 14001:2015, and ISO 14068-1:2023 certifications, along with carbon neutrality accreditation, forming a comprehensive integrated management system that ensures the highest standards of quality, information security, and environmental responsibility.

Environmental Management & Climate Action

ISO 14001:2015 is the internationally recognised standard for Environmental Management Systems (EMS), providing a framework for organisations to design, implement, and continually improve their environmental performance. This standard helps us minimise our environmental footprint, comply with relevant legal requirements, and achieve our environmental objectives. ISO 14001 helps control environmental aspects, reduce impacts and ensure legal compliance, supporting our commitment to sustainable operations and resource efficiency. You can view our certificate here

ISO 14068-1:2023 is the latest international standard for carbon neutrality management, replacing the previous PAS 2060 standard. This standard provides principles, requirements, and guidance for achieving and demonstrating carbon neutrality through quantifying, reducing, and offsetting carbon footprints, utilising a hierarchical approach that prioritises direct and indirect greenhouse gas emission reductions over offsetting. The standard ensures that carbon neutrality efforts are true, fair, science-based and transparent, providing a structured approach that supports the journey to carbon neutral/net zero. You can view our report here.

Carbon Neutrality represents our commitment to achieving net-zero carbon emissions by balancing the greenhouse gases we emit with equivalent amounts removed from the atmosphere. This involves adopting sustainable practices, working with sustainable suppliers, investing in emission reduction technologies, and supporting verified carbon offset projects to minimise our net contribution to greenhouse gases in the environment. Our carbon neutrality accreditation demonstrates our proactive approach to climate action and environmental responsibility.

 

Environmental Policy

Purpose of the Environmental Policy 

Phew Design Limited (Phew) is committed to minimising the environmental impact of our operations by integrating sustainable practices into all aspects of the business. This environmental policy is designed to ensure Phew’s operations align with our strategic objectives and meet our environmental obligations, including those outlined by the ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management System (EMS).

Scope 

This policy applies to all activities, products, and services across Phew’s operations, including the development and delivery of digital solutions for councils, healthcare providers, educational institutions, and safeguarding organisations.

Commitments 

Phew’s top management has established, implemented and maintain an environmental policy and makes the following commitments:

  • Ensuring its activities are safe for its employees, associates, clients and others who come into contact with his work.
  • Complying with all legal requirements
  • Accepting reasonable responsibility for any harm to the environment caused by its activities and taking reasonable steps to remedy any damage
  • Monitoring purchasing practice and internal operations, including energy and transport, to ensure best use of natural resources and minimum environmental impact.
  • Whenever possible, reduce the environmental impact of goods and services supplied by adopting a ‘cradle – to – grave’ assessment and responsibilities for them
  • Minimizing the waste produced in all parts of the business
  • Monitoring and working with its suppliers and other third parties associated with its business and setting them similar high standards
  • Seeking to integrate environmental considerations into further business policies and decisions
  • Ensuring associates understand and are accountable to policy goals throughout communications and training
  • Communicating the policy as appropriate to customers and suppliers
  • Developing systems to implement and review this policy

Our Purpose and Context  

The Environmental policy is appropriate to the purpose, context, and the nature, scale, and environmental impacts of Phew’s activities, products, and services. As a digital solutions provider, Phew focuses on reducing energy consumption in IT infrastructure, optimising resource use, and preventing environmental impacts associated with electronic waste.

Environmental Objectives 

This policy provides a framework for setting measurable and actionable environmental objectives that are reviewed regularly. Phew are committed to:

  • Reducing energy consumption and emissions associated with digital operations.
  • Minimising waste generation and improving recycling rates.
  • Advancing sustainable IT practices to enhance environmental performance.
  • Measuring, reducing and minimising our scope 1, 2 and 3 carbon emissions arising from our activities.

 

Our Commitment to Environmental Protection  

Phew are committed to protecting the environment, which includes:

  • Prevention of Pollution: Implementing practices to avoid pollution in all aspects of operations.
  • Sustainable Resource Use: Promoting efficient use of natural resources, particularly in procurement and IT services.
  • Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation: Reducing greenhouse gas emissions by leveraging energy-efficient systems and adopting innovative strategies to adapt to climate change challenges.
  • Protection of Environmental biodiversity and Ecosystems: Ensuring business activities respect the environment and support the preservation of biodiversity.

Compliance with Legal and Regulatory Obligations 

Phew are dedicated to fulfilling all compliance obligations. This includes adherence to local, national, and international environmental regulations and agreements to ensure full legal compliance.

Commitment to Continual Improvement

Phew are committed to continually improving our Environmental Management System to enhance overall environmental performance. Regular leadership reviews and evaluations of environmental practices drive innovation and sustainable development.

Documentation and Communication 

To ensure transparency and engagement, Phew will:

  • Document: Maintain this environmental policy as documented information within the EMS.
  • Communicate: Ensure the policy is effectively communicated across the company to foster awareness and support for environmental commitments.
  • Make Available: Provide this policy to all relevant stakeholders, including customers, suppliers, and regulatory bodies upon request.

Phew’s environmental policy reflects our strong commitment to environmental stewardship, compliance, and continual improvement. Through sustainable practices, efficient resource use, and robust environmental management, the company strives to protect the environment while creating value for stakeholders.

Cyber Essentials Plus Certifications

Phew have attained Cyber Essentials Plus certification, reinforcing our dedication to maintaining robust cybersecurity practices. This certification reflects our ongoing commitment to safeguarding our systems and data, underscoring our proactive approach to cybersecurity.  

By achieving Cyber Essentials Plus certification, we demonstrate our capability to mitigate cyber risks effectively and uphold the highest standards of security across our operations, solidifying our position as a trusted partner, assuring our clients and stakeholders of our unwavering commitment to cybersecurity excellence. 

You can view our certificate here. 

G Cloud Framework

Phew is proud to be an approved supplier on the G-Cloud Digital Marketplace, making it easier for public sector organisations to access our digital solutions. This government framework streamlines procurement processes, allowing schools, councils, and other public bodies to quickly find and purchase our audit systems, learning management platforms, and website services without lengthy tender processes.

External Assessments & Audits

Phew conducts penetration tests and security assessments on its public-facing and internal infrastructure and application services through accredited third-party experts, using CREST certified penetration testers. Vulnerabilities identified during these assessments are promptly reported to Phew and subsequently addressed and tracked in accordance with industry best practices. 

Furthermore, Phew engages Paul Robinson Management Services to perform impartial and professional external audits of its Integrated Management System (IMS) governance and security programmes. 

Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity

Phew upholds a comprehensive Disaster Recovery (DR) plan designed to fortify our business continuity strategy for critical production services, systems, and platforms. Developed from industry-recognised methodologies, including ISO27000 standards. 

Regular assessments against stringent regulatory and governance requirements ensure the effectiveness of our Disaster Recovery plan. Additionally, Phew conducts scheduled fire drills to continuously test and improve the efficiency of our DR procedures in a proactive cycle of enhancement.
